The Frequency Hopper Sequencer

Frequency hopping also known as frequency hopping spread spectrum (FHSS)
is a method of transmitting radio signals by rapidly switching the carrier between
different frequency channels. Switching occurs based on a pseudo-random sequence
known to the transmitter and the receiver.

Inspired by this method, my project simulates the 'hopping' between frequencies,
in a way the musical sequencer does. Unfortunatelly, live sampling of a web SDR,
which receives the sounds I'm interested in, is very hard to achieve.
The user can sequence their own melodies, with the limited 'tuning'; a representattion
of bandwith shift. If the live sampling was possible, this would result in a much broader
range of available sounds.

This project invites the user to forget about the specific frequencies; they become
invisible and irrelevant. It encourages whoever decides to play along to tune in to the
new musical emergence by ear and use their creativity to turn a meaningless radio noise
into their own sonic event.
